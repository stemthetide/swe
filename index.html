Learn the Facts

1. Sea levels are rising at the rate of over 1/8 inches per year.

2. Flooding is approximately 300% to 900% more frequent within US coastal communities than it was just 50 years ago.

3. In the US 40% of the population lives in high population density coastal areas where sea levels influence flooding, shoreline erosion, and storm hazards.

4. Globally 8 of the 10 largest cities are near coasts according to the UN Oceans Atlas.

5. The 2 major causes of global sea level rise are 1) thermal expansion and melting ice caused by warming of the ocean since water expands as it warms and 2) increased carbon emissions from humans that trap heat in the environment.

6. The oceans are absorbing over 90% of the increased atmospheric heat associated with emissions from human activity.

Minimize Driving Time

Carpool, bike, drive at steady speeds,
and maintain your tire inflation to save over 1 ton of CO2 annually.

Smart Home

Invest in programmable thermostats,
LED lighting, insulation, and energy usage reduction whenever possible.

Conscious Eating

Cancel your junk mail, buy locally,
eat less meat, and do not waste food!

Reuse, Reduce, Recyle

Reuse items you need often and invest in recyclable items to reduce CO2 emissions by 30%.

Stay Informed. Be Engaged.

Vote on infrastructure policy and legislation like seawalls and salt resistant vegetation.
